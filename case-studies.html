People Helping People — Volunteering Platform

Project Overview:

"People Helping People" is a web-based platform designed to connect individuals in need of help with volunteers offering assistance. The website allows users to request and offer help for various tasks, such as mowing lawns, tech support, or helping with groceries, to name a few examples.

Why It Exists:

This idea stemmed from my professor, with the goal of teaching us what UX designers go through and the process of how to make great designs. It also aimed to show us the complexities involved in making a website accessible and thoughtful—are we adding features just to have them, or is there real business value behind each one?

Key Features:

  • Task Request System: Easy-to-use forms for users to request help with tasks.
  • Volunteer Dashboard: Volunteers can filter tasks based on skills, location, and availability.
  • Volunteer Profiles: Users can view profiles of volunteers, see their skills, and get in contact with them.

Technologies Used:

HTML, CSS, JavaScript, Figma

What I Learned:

This project taught me a lot about working in groups and managing my time, as design takes a lot of thought and consideration. I learned how to use Figma to create a design system component library. I also learned how much effort it takes to develop a website with design in mind and what stakeholders look for in a design.

Artisan Grit — E-Commerce Web-site

Project Overview:

Artisan Grit is an e-commerce website focused on distributing streetwear clothing with custom designs.

Why It Exists:

I have also had a passion for creating, this business allows me to fulfill that createive drive by putting out custome desings on products I know people will love to wear, at an affordable price.

Key Features:

  • Product Listings with Artisan Stories: Each product page includes a story about the creator and their craft.
  • Streamlined Filtering System: Users can search by material, price, or occasion to find the perfect items.
  • Minimalist UI: Clean, image-centric design focused on showcasing the products.

Technologies Used:

Vue.js, Node.js, Stripe API

What I Learned:

This project deepened my understanding of front-end development and taught me how to approach building a business and being resourceful when facing new challenges—like finding a manufacturer that doesn’t overwhelm us with costs, and choosing the right material to showcase our designs.

Shopping_ListApp — Soon to be known as Pantry Planner

Project Overview:

Pantry Planner is a web application with aspirations to become a mobile app. It helps users plan healthy meals, generate grocery shopping lists, and manage their budgets. The app provides personalized meal suggestions based on ingredients the user already has.

Why It Exists:

The app was designed to help users eat healthier by making meal planning easier and more efficient. With many people struggling to create healthy meal plans on a budget, this app simplifies the process by generating shopping lists based on previous shopping history, dietary goals, and available ingredients.

Key Features:

  • Smart Shopping List: Automatically generates grocery lists based on preferred recipes and pantry stock.
  • Personalized Meal Suggestions: Recommends meals based on past purchases and dietary preferences.
  • Budgeting Tool: Tracks spending and offers budget-friendly meal ideas.

Technologies Used:

Currently: Vue.js, JavaScript
Planned stack change: Vue.js, Python, MongoDB

What I Learned:

This is a fairly new application, so I am still in the process of figuring things out. I've had to change the plan a couple of times based on feedback I received from pitching my idea at a few startup clubs.
